# Executive Report — Revenue Trends, Customer Behavior, and Category Performance

## 1. Overview

This report analyzes revenue trends, customer behavior, and product category performance over the past 12 months. The goal is to identify growth drivers, retention patterns, and opportunities to support strategic decision-making.

---

## 2. Cohort Analysis (Customer Acquisition)

Customers were grouped based on their first purchase month to analyze acquisition patterns.

* The number of new customers increases steadily from April through December.
* The highest cohort size is observed in December, reaching 600 customers.
* A sharp decline occurs in January, dropping to 275 customers.
* Customer acquisition recovers in February after the seasonal decline.
* March shows a lower cohort size, likely due to incomplete data for the month.

**Key Insight:**
Customer acquisition is strongly influenced by seasonality, with peak acquisition during Q4 and a post-holiday decline in Q1.

---

## 3. Revenue Growth Analysis

Revenue shows strong growth throughout the year, particularly in Q3 and Q4.

* Revenue increased significantly between Q3 and Q4, with growth exceeding 100% in some periods.
* The increase was primarily driven by a rise in order volume rather than higher spending per order.
* A sharp decline occurred in January, followed by recovery in February and March.

**Key Insight:**
Revenue growth is mainly driven by transaction volume, not price increases.

---

## 4. Trend Analysis (Moving Averages)

Moving averages were used to smooth daily fluctuations and reveal underlying trends.

* The 7-day and 30-day moving averages show a steady upward trend from April to September.
* A clear peak appears in Q4, indicating strong seasonal demand.
* A noticeable drop occurs in January, followed by gradual recovery.

**Key Insight:**
Daily data is highly volatile, but moving averages reveal consistent long-term growth and strong seasonality patterns.

---

## 5. Category Performance Analysis

Revenue distribution across categories shows a strong concentration in one category.

* Electronics consistently accounts for more than 55% of total revenue.

* Other categories (Sports, Home & Kitchen, Clothing) contribute moderately.

* Books remain a very small contributor (~1%).

* All categories experienced strong growth in Q4.

* All categories declined sharply in January, indicating a market-wide seasonal effect.

* Recovery is visible across all categories in February and March.

**Key Insights:**

* The business is heavily dependent on Electronics.
* Revenue fluctuations are driven by overall demand rather than individual category performance.
* Limited diversification presents a potential risk.

---

## 6. Key Business Insights

* Growth is driven primarily by increased order volume.
* Strong seasonality impacts performance, especially during Q4 and post-holiday periods.
* Revenue is highly concentrated in a single category (Electronics).
* Customer acquisition is seasonal and peaks during high-demand periods.

---

## 7. Recommendations

* **Diversification Strategy:** Invest in growing underperforming categories to reduce dependency on Electronics.
* **Retention Programs:** Implement targeted campaigns to improve repeat purchases.
* **Seasonal Planning:** Leverage Q4 demand and prepare strategies to mitigate Q1 decline.
* **Customer Growth:** Focus on increasing customer acquisition during off-peak periods.

---

## 8. Conclusion

The business demonstrates strong growth driven by demand and seasonality. However, reliance on a single category and seasonal acquisition patterns present opportunities for strategic improvement. Addressing these areas can lead to more balanced and sustainable growth.
